Problem & Mission Statement

For our P33 project we teamed up with M1 to bring awareness to and teach about an economic issue that many people face.

Many potential electric car owners are scared off by the high initial cost compared to gas cars. What we want to teach is that owning an electric vehicle is a long-term investment, and over time the economic benefits become more apparent.

Our goal became to create a Java program that shows long-term ownership costs, along with this website to teach important facts about electric vehicles.

What is an Electric Vehicle? & Different Types

The most common Electric Vehicle is a Battery-Electric Vehicle (BEV), which runs entirely on electricity.

Hybrid vehicles combine electric motors with gasoline engines. Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEV) can be charged by plugging in, while Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEV) recharge using the gasoline engine.

Costs of Conventional Cars vs. Electric Cars

Cost to Charge

The average annual cost of charging an EV is about $755 compared to $1,615 for gasoline vehicles.

Driving Range

Many modern EVs can travel over 300 miles on a single charge, with fast charging available.

Maintenance Costs

EVs typically require less maintenance. A Tesla Model 3 costs about $190 per year to maintain, while a Toyota RAV4 costs about $964.

Electric Vehicle Chargers Explained

EVs can be charged at home or at public charging stations.

Charging costs vary depending on location, time of day, and energy provider. Electricity can come from renewable or non-renewable sources.
